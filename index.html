Project Overview

For our first project, we were tasked with creating a web application containing three buttons. Each button was designed to represent a team member’s personality or likeness in a unique way. Interacting with the buttons highlighted relationships between team members, showcasing similarities in our experiences, values, and personalities.

My Role

For this project, my main role focused on generating design ideas and exploring different concepts through sketches and group discussions. I also took on a lead development role, creating a simple web framework that allowed my team members to easily integrate their own code, assets, and animations. In addition, I designed and created the background asset using Sprite Fusion (a tilemap editor), incorporating input from all group members.

Design Process & Philosphy

As this was our first project, both our design process and my personal design philosophy were relatively simple and novice. At this stage, my understanding of design was largely centered around the 10×10 ideation method, as that was all we had covered. I focused on generating a range of unique concepts through sketches during a brief divergence phase.

After developing initial ideas, we came together as a group to converge and selected a concept based on preference rather than using a structured evaluation. We then made small adjustments to the chosen design, refining elements slightly based on aesthetics rather than functionality or the underlying meanings associated with the project goal.

This reflects my early design approach that prioritized idea generation through simple sketches and selecting a workable concept that we thought was "good" enough, rather than systematically evaluating, justifying, or iterating on specific designs.

Project Overview

For our second project, we were tasked with creating a system that helps experts in 3D CAD software synchronously and remotely share expertise with novice users. The system also needed to incorporate novel uses of embodied input and output to effectively convey and receive this expertise.

My Role

For this project, I stepped back from a lead development role and focused on generating and refining design concepts. I contributed through sketch based ideation and developed a higher-fidelity concept using Fusion 360 and 3D printing. I also supported the team by assisting with implementation and documentation as needed.

Design Process & Philosophy

Building on feedback from our initial design critique, I began to question whether simply generating ideas was enough to arrive at an effective design. While the 10×10 method supported strong idea generation, it did not ensure that the selected concept was the "right design".

Once again, we began with a divergence phase, where each team member generated a range of concepts using the 10×10 method. During convergence, we initially fell into a similar pattern as in Project 1, selecting a concept based on preference rather than structured evaluation or comparison against defined criteria.

After developing an initial prototype and discussing it as a group, I recognized that we had repeated the same limitation from our previous approach. We had gotten tunnle visioned on identifying a workable concept rather than ensuring it was the most suitable one. As a result, we chose to diverge again, generating new ideas and reconsidering our options more carefully.

During this second convergence phase, we introduced a more structured approach by defining criteria and ranking concepts against one another. This marked a shift in my design philosophy, moving toward more intentional decision making and justifying design choices rather than relying solely on personal preference.

Although this was a step in the right direction, I recognized that my design approach was still limited, as I continued to neglect the use of other design process models that we had covered in class. While we introduced structured evaluation to identify what we believed to be the “right” design, we were still lacking individual design iteration and refinement. My use of the 10×10 method remained focused on generating ideas rather than iterating on and improving those ideas.
